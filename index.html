# Shepherd Research Lab **Innovation in bridging computational imaging science and cancer research.** The Shepherd Research Lab is a research group led by Dr. John A. Shepherd at the [University of Hawaiʻi Cancer Center](https://www.uhcancercenter.org/). Our studies include breast cancer detection through breast density, predicting health risks through body composition assessments, and utilizing deep learning for AI-assisted diagnosis. [Visit the Body Composition Lab website ↗](https://bcepem.shepherdresearchlab.org/)
## Aloha! At SRL, we are a group of research scientists innovating the ways we detect cancer and metabolic risk. Along with how this benefits all areas of health, we are especially proud of the benefits our studies can bring to our local community on Oʻahu. ## Research & Innovation Our work includes a number of active human studies and technology development projects: - Predicting breast cancer risk from breast density and tissue thickness - Using body analysis and imaging technology to predict metabolic health status and disease risk - Utilizing deep learning to improve AI-assisted diagnosis ![Body composition predictions across automatic and manual methods]({{ site.baseurl }}/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/Tian-Fig-6.jpg) ![3D avatars showing body composition measurement]({{ site.baseurl }}/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/wong-2022-obesity-fig1.jpg)

## Featured Research: Three-Compartment Breast Lesion Detection Leong LT, Malkov S, Drukker K, Niell BL, Sadowski P, Wolfgruber T, et al. [Dual-energy three-compartment breast imaging for compositional biomarkers to improve detection of malignant lesions.](https://www.nature.com/articles/s43856-021-00024-0) *Communications Medicine.* 2021 Aug 31;1(1):29. ![Improved ROC performance when adding 3CB compositional information]({{ site.baseurl }}/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/Leong_commmed_fig2.png) Adding three-compartment breast (3CB) features to computer-aided detection (CAD) yields an area under the ROC curve of **0.81** — compared to **0.69** for CAD alone. The integrated discrimination improvement demonstrates substantial benefit from the addition of 3CB features.
