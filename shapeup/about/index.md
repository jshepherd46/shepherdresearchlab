Shape Up! is a collaborative research initiative spanning four institutions:

- **University of Hawaiʻi Cancer Center** — Honolulu (lead site)
- **Pennington Biomedical Research Center** — Louisiana State University, Baton Rouge
- **University of California, San Francisco** — UCSF
- **University of Washington** — Department of Computer Science and Engineering, Seattle

## Purpose

The study explores how to measure health and body composition from 2D and 3D images. Researchers use external body-shape data to predict internal health measures — body-fat percentage, lean muscle mass, and other indicators that help assess health and wellness risk.

## Research goal

Build the largest and most powerful description of optical body shape and its association with body composition, metabolic markers, function, and dietary intake.

## Funding

Three NIH grants support the research:

- **R01 DK109008** — Optical Body Composition and Health Assessment (NIDDK)
- **R01 DK111698** — Shape Up! Kids (NIDDK)
- **R01 HD103885** — Quantifying body shape in pediatric clinical research (NICHD)
