## What is a DXA scan?

Dual-energy X-ray Absorptiometry (DXA) uses small amounts of x-ray to measure your body composition. A DXA scan can be used to:

- quantify and locate body fat percentage and lean muscle mass percentage in your body
- measure bone mineral density
- monitor bone density and body composition trends over time

This scan is FDA-approved to quantify the deep, dangerous fat that surrounds your organs — visceral adipose tissue (VAT) — which is associated with type-2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and cancer.

## How should I prepare for a DXA scan?

Avoid calcium supplements 24 hours before a DXA scan. A lighter meal or fasting beforehand is recommended.

You will change into a gown and remove zips, hooks, jewelry, or buckles.

## What can I expect at a DXA scan?

You will lie down on a table and a scanning arm will pass over your body. The process typically takes less than 10 minutes.

You receive a detailed report at the end of your scan.
