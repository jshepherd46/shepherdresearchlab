![ARCS Scholars 2024]({{ site.baseurl }}/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/manoa-arcs-scholars-group-2024.jpg)

Sixteen STEM doctoral students at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa received recognition for outstanding research accomplishments. **Alika Maunakea** from the John A. Burns School of Medicine earned the **2024 Scientist of the Year** award for his contributions to epigenetics research.

Among the honored scholars was **Arianna Bunnell**, studying computer and information science. Her research focuses on "Developing AI-equipped ultrasound devices for breast cancer screening."
